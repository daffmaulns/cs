### The idea

Learning isn’t what you read.
It’s what you can still do when you’re tired, rushed, and slightly afraid.

Archiv is built for that version of you.

You bring the question.
You try.
You write what you remember, what you suspect, what you’d do next.

Archiv doesn’t jump in to finish the thought.
It shapes it.
With questions. With pressure. With just enough guidance.

The result is not a prettier answer on the screen.
It’s a more reliable one in your head.

---

### A session in Archiv

You open a problem.
Archiv asks what you see first.

You sketch a path.
Archiv pushes on the weak points.

You adjust, refine, commit.
Only then does it unfold the full solution — aligned with the work you’ve already done.

Later, it brings the idea back.
Different numbers. Different framing. Same concept.
Until your response feels less like recall and more like reflex.

---

### Built for pressure

Archiv trains you for the moments when there is no pause button.

Exam rooms.
Technical interviews.
Presentations where the follow-up questions matter more than the slides.

It recreates the feeling of being on the spot —
but in a place where it’s safe to get it wrong, refine, and go again.

You’re not rewarded for guessing what the AI wants to hear.
You’re rewarded for building a pattern your brain can trust when it’s alone.

---

### The rhythm

Archiv works best when it’s part of your day.

Short sessions.
A handful of hard questions.
Enough friction to leave a trace.

It tracks what sticks and what slips.
It returns to the weak links at the right time.
Not to remind you what a formula looks like —
but to ask your mind to rebuild it, one more time.

---

### What Archiv is made for

Concepts that crack under stress.

Limits, proofs, derivations.
Mechanics, circuits, algorithms.
Any field where understanding fails loudly when it isn’t real.

Archiv doesn’t care how pretty your notes are.
It cares what survives when the page is gone.

### Philosophy

There will always be tools that will do the work for you.
Archiv exists for the people who still want the work.

Not to make study easy.
To make performance honest.

When everything around you is optimized for convenience,
Archiv is optimized for capability.

So when it’s time to answer —
in the room, in the call, in the moment —

you don’t rise to the occasion.
You fall back to training.
